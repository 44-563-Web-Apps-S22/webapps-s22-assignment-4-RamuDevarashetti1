The Learned Women

ACT II : Leaving CLITANDRE and still speaking to him

SCENE I; CHRYSALE, ARISTE.

ARI

Good day to you, brother.

CHRY

And to you also, brother.

ARI

Do you know what brings me here?

CHRY

No, I do not; but I am ready to hear it, if it pleases you to tell me.

ARI

You have known Clitandre for some time now?

CHRY

Certainly; and he often comes to our house.

ARI

And what do you think of him?

CHRY

I think him to be a man of honour, wit, courage, and uprightness, and I know very few people who have more merit.

ARI

A certain wish of his has brought me here; and I am glad to see the esteem you have for him.

CHRY

I became acquainted with his late father when I was in Rome.

ARI

Ah!

CHRY

He was a perfect gentleman.
